<p><p>The RAID disk array market consists of various configurations catering to diverse storage needs. RAID 0 offers high performance through striping but lacks redundancy. RAID 1 provides data mirroring for reliability. RAID 5 balances performance and fault tolerance with parity across drives. RAID 6 enhances this with double parity, ensuring higher data protection. RAID 10 combines mirroring and striping for optimal performance and redundancy. RAID 50 merges RAID 5's striping with RAID 0's performance, while other RAID types cater to specialized use cases.</p></p>
