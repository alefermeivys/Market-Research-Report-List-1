<p><p>The Hepatitis B vaccination market is primarily segmented into yeast-derived and CHO (Chinese Hamster Ovary) cell-derived vaccines. Yeast-derived vaccines utilize recombinant DNA technology to produce the hepatitis B surface antigen in yeast cells, offering a safe and effective immunization method. In contrast, CHO-derived vaccines use mammalian cells to produce the antigen, providing advantages in post-translational modifications and higher yields. Both types are crucial for controlling hepatitis B infections and are widely implemented in vaccination programs globally.</p></p>
