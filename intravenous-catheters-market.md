<p><p>The intravenous catheters market comprises three main types: Peripheral Catheters, Midline Peripheral Catheters, and Central Venous Catheters. Peripheral Catheters are typically used for short-term access and are inserted into smaller veins. Midline Peripheral Catheters are longer than peripheral catheters, suitable for intermediate-term therapy, and are inserted into larger veins, offering increased stability. Central Venous Catheters are designed for long-term use and are inserted into larger central veins, allowing for the administration of medications, fluids, and blood products.</p></p>
