<p><p>In the IoT market, 5G technology is implemented in two primary architectures: 5G NR Standalone (SA) and 5G NR Non-Standalone (NSA). The Standalone architecture utilizes a complete 5G core network, allowing for ultra-low latency and high reliability, essential for advanced IoT applications. Conversely, Non-Standalone architecture leverages existing 4G infrastructure alongside 5G components, facilitating a smoother transition to 5G while enhancing current IoT services. Both architectures support diverse IoT use cases, including smart cities, industrial automation, and connected vehicles.</p></p>
