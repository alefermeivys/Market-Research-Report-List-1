<p><p>The influenza vaccine market is primarily divided into two types: trivalent and quadrivalent vaccines. Trivalent influenza vaccines target three virus strains, typically covering two A strains and one B strain, providing essential protection against common circulating strains. In contrast, quadrivalent vaccines include an additional B strain, enhancing coverage and immunity against a broader range of viruses. This expanded protection is particularly beneficial in years when multiple B strains are prevalent, making quadrivalent vaccines increasingly popular among healthcare providers and patients.</p></p>
