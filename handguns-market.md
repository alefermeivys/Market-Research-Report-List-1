<p><p>The handgun market consists of three primary types: automatic, semi-automatic, and manual. Automatic handguns fire continuously as long as the trigger is held, offering rapid firing capability. Semi-automatic handguns discharge one round per trigger pull, utilizing the energy from each shot to eject the spent casing and chamber a new round. Manual handguns, such as revolvers, require the shooter to manually operate the mechanism to fire successive rounds. Each type serves distinct user preferences and application needs in various contexts.</p></p>
